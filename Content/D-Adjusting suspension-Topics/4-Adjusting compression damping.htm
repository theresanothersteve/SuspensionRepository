Adjusting compression damping

It is hard to set compression damping statically.

If you have no other data, the best thing is to set compression damping halfway between fully closed and fully open.

Fork compression damping

Compression damping might be adjusted by a screw on the bottom of each fork leg, a screw on the top of each fork cap, or on one fork (while rebound is on the other).

Shock compression damping

Shock compression damping is usually on the big end, or canister end, of the rear damper.

Compression damping adjusters are usually marked COMP.

If you have another trusted setting, such as that from the owner’s manual, you can use that as a starting point.

Write down the settings you decided on.

Place a zip tie around the right-hand fork leg. When the suspension compresses, it will push the zip tie down. When you stop, you can see how much travel has been used during the ride.

Öhlins also have a spring retained wiper that looks neater but is more expensive than a zip tie. The zip tie does the same job.
